# Organic Chemistry II: Hydrocarbon Reactions and Mechanisms

## 1. Electrophilic Addition to Alkenes

### Hydrohalogenation (Markovnikov's Rule)
When an asymmetrical alkene reacts with a hydrogen halide (HX), the hydrogen atom adds to the carbon with the greater number of hydrogen atoms, yielding the more stable carbocation intermediate.

**Reaction Mechanism: Addition of HBr to Propene**
1. **Protonation**: The alkene $\pi$-bond attacks the electrophilic proton of HBr, forming a secondary carbocation.
   $$ \text{CH}_3\text{-CH=CH}_2 + \text{H-Br} \rightarrow [\text{CH}_3\text{-C}^+\text{H-CH}_3] + \text{Br}^- $$
2. **Nucleophilic Attack**: The bromide ion attacks the carbocation to form 2-bromopropane.
   $$ [\text{CH}_3\text{-C}^+\text{H-CH}_3] + \text{Br}^- \rightarrow \text{CH}_3\text{-CHBr-CH}_3 $$

**Worked Example 1:**
Predict the major product for the reaction of 1-methylcyclohexene with HCl.

*Solution:*
1. 1-methylcyclohexene is protonated at the less substituted $C_2$ carbon.
2. This creates a tertiary carbocation at $C_1$.
3. Chloride ion attacks the tertiary carbocation.
4. The major product is 1-chloro-1-methylcyclohexane.

### Hydration and Halogenation
- **Acid-Catalyzed Hydration**: Addition of water across an alkene in the presence of an acid catalyst (e.g., $H_2SO_4$). Yields a Markovnikov alcohol.
- **Halogenation**: Addition of $Br_2$ or $Cl_2$. Proceeds via a cyclic halonium ion intermediate, resulting in anti-addition.

## 2. Substitution and Elimination in Hydrocarbons

While alkanes undergo radical substitution, alkyl halides (frequently derived from hydrocarbons) undergo nucleophilic substitution (SN1/SN2) and elimination (E1/E2).

### E2 Elimination Mechanism
A concerted mechanism where a strong base removes a $\beta$-proton simultaneously with the leaving group's departure, forming a double bond.

**Worked Example 2:**
Reaction of 2-bromo-2-methylbutane with sodium ethoxide ($NaOCH_2CH_3$).

*Solution:*
1. Substrate: Tertiary alkyl halide.
2. Reagent: Strong base ($EtO^-$).
3. Pathway: E2 predominates due to steric hindrance preventing SN2 and the strong base driving elimination.
4. Products: Zaitsev product (2-methyl-2-butene) is favored over the Hofmann product (2-methyl-1-butene) because the base is unhindered.

## 3. Reactions of Alkynes

### Hydration of Alkynes
Terminal alkynes undergo hydration to form enols, which rapidly tautomerize to ketones (via acid catalysis) or aldehydes (via hydroboration-oxidation).

- **Acid-Catalyzed Hydration** (Markovnikov): Uses $HgSO_4/H_2SO_4$. Yields a methyl ketone.
- **Hydroboration-Oxidation** (Anti-Markovnikov): Uses bulky boranes (e.g., sia_2BH) followed by $H_2O_2/NaOH$. Yields an aldehyde.
