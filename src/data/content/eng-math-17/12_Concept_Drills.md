```drill
Question: You calculate the dot product of a force vector and a velocity vector and get a negative number. What does this mean geometrically?
Options:
A) The two vectors are perpendicular.
B) The angle between the two vectors is acute (less than 90 degrees).
C) The angle between the two vectors is obtuse (greater than 90 degrees).
D) The two vectors are parallel.
Answer: C
Explanation: The geometric definition of the dot product is $\|\mathbf{a}\|\|\mathbf{b}\|\cos\theta$. Because magnitudes are always positive, the sign of the dot product is determined entirely by $\cos\theta$. Cosine is negative in the second quadrant ($90^\circ < \theta \le 180^\circ$).
```

```drill
Question: You have two non-zero vectors, $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$. You calculate their cross product and find that $\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b} = \mathbf{0}$. What must be true?
Options:
A) They are perpendicular (orthogonal).
B) They are parallel (or anti-parallel).
C) They are unit vectors.
D) They are coplanar.
Answer: B
Explanation: The magnitude of the cross product is $\|\mathbf{a}\|\|\mathbf{b}\|\sin\theta$. For this to equal zero (without the vectors themselves being zero), $\sin\theta$ must be zero. This occurs at $\theta = 0^\circ$ (parallel) or $\theta = 180^\circ$ (anti-parallel).
```

```drill
Question: You are given the equation of a plane: $4x - 2y + 7z = 15$. What is a vector that is perfectly perpendicular to this plane?
Options:
A) $\langle 4, -2, 7 \rangle$
B) $\langle -4, 2, -7 \rangle$
C) Both A and B.
D) You need a point on the plane to find out.
Answer: C
Explanation: In the standard linear equation of a plane $ax + by + cz = d$, the coefficients $a, b, c$ are exactly the components of the normal vector. Therefore, $\langle 4, -2, 7 \rangle$ is a normal vector. Its exact opposite $\langle -4, 2, -7 \rangle$ is also perfectly perpendicular, just pointing out the back of the plane.
```

```drill
Question: In Spherical Coordinates $(\rho, \theta, \phi)$, what 3D geometric shape is created by setting the angle $\phi$ to a constant value of $\pi/4$ (45 degrees)?
Options:
A) A flat horizontal plane.
B) A sphere.
C) A cylinder.
D) An infinitely tall cone.
Answer: D
Explanation: $\phi$ is the angle measured downward from the positive z-axis. If you lock $\phi = 45^\circ$, but allow the radius $\rho$ to stretch to infinity and the horizontal angle $\theta$ to spin 360 degrees, you sweep out a perfect cone opening upwards.
```
