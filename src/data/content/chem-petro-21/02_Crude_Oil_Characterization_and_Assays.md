# Crude Oil Characterization and Assays

You cannot operate a \$5 Billion refinery blind. Before a refinery purchases a 2-million-barrel supertanker of crude oil from overseas, the engineers must know exactly what is inside that specific oil. 

Every single oil field on earth produces a chemically unique crude oil. "West Texas Intermediate" (WTI) is vastly different from "Saudi Arab Light," which is vastly different from "Canadian Cold Lake."

To understand a specific oil, chemists perform an exhaustive, multi-week laboratory analysis called a **Crude Assay**.

## 1. The Global Benchmarks

The financial markets price crude oil based on two primary, rapid physical measurements: Density and Sulfur Content.

### API Gravity (Density)
The petroleum industry uses a bizarre, inverse density scale created by the American Petroleum Institute (API).
*   **High API ($> 35^\circ$):** "Light" crude oil. It is thin, highly fluid, and packed with valuable gasoline and diesel molecules. It is extremely expensive and desirable.
*   **Low API ($< 20^\circ$):** "Heavy" crude oil. It is thick, viscous, and packed with heavy, worthless asphalt. It trades at a massive discount because it requires a multi-billion dollar "Coking" refinery to process.

### Sulfur Content (Sweet vs. Sour)
*   **Sweet Crude (< 0.5% Sulfur):** Incredibly valuable. Requires very little expensive hydrogen treating in the refinery to meet environmental regulations.
*   **Sour Crude (> 1.5% Sulfur):** Cheaper, but highly toxic. The refinery must spend millions of dollars operating massive Hydrotreating units to violently rip the sulfur out of the oil.

*Example:* **Brent Crude** (the European benchmark) is a Light, Sweet crude ($38^\circ$ API, 0.4% Sulfur). It is the gold standard for easy refining.

## 2. The True Boiling Point (TBP) Curve

API and Sulfur only give a rough estimate. To actually design the massive distillation towers of the refinery, the chemical engineers require a **True Boiling Point (TBP) Curve**.

In a highly controlled laboratory, chemists take a 5-gallon sample of the crude oil and slowly boil it over several days. They precisely measure exactly what percentage of the oil boils off at every single temperature.

The result is a curve that tells the refinery manager exactly how much of each product is hidden inside the barrel:
1.  **Light Ends (Boils below $90^\circ\text{F}$):** Methane, Ethane, Propane, Butane. (Typically 2-5%).
2.  **Naphtha / Straight Run Gasoline (Boils $90 - 400^\circ\text{F}$):** The absolute most valuable slice. Directly blended into car gasoline or sent to chemical plants to make plastics. (Typically 15-30%).
3.  **Kerosene / Jet Fuel (Boils $400 - 500^\circ\text{F}$):** Highly refined fuel for aviation turbines. (Typically 10-15%).
4.  **Diesel / Light Gas Oil (Boils $500 - 650^\circ\text{F}$):** Fuel for trucks, trains, and ships. (Typically 15-20%).
5.  **Heavy Gas Oil (Boils $650 - 1050^\circ\text{F}$):** Too heavy to be used as fuel. This is the primary "feedstock" that will be violently cracked in half by the Fluid Catalytic Cracker (FCC) to artificially make more gasoline.
6.  **Vacuum Residue (Boils above $1050^\circ\text{F}$):** The "Bottom of the Barrel." Rock-solid asphalt and tar. It will never boil at atmospheric pressure. 

## 3. Advanced Characterization (PONA)

The TBP curve tells you *how much* gasoline you have. But it does not tell you the *quality* of that gasoline.

A car engine requires High-Octane fuel to prevent catastrophic engine knocking. Octane is dictated entirely by the microscopic molecular shape of the hydrocarbons.
*   Straight-chain Paraffins have a terrible Octane rating (near zero).
*   Branched Isoparaffins and Aromatics have an astronomical Octane rating (near 100).

The laboratory chemists use advanced Gas Chromatography and Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS) to chemically identify every single molecule in the naphtha slice. This is the **PONA Analysis** (Paraffins, Olefins, Naphthenes, Aromatics).

If the Assay reveals that the raw gasoline is 90% straight-chain paraffins, the refinery engineer knows it is garbage. They cannot sell it to a gas station. They must route it into a massive "Catalytic Reforming" unit, which uses Platinum catalysts to physically bend the straight molecules into high-octane rings before it can be legally sold as fuel.
