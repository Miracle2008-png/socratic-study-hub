# Concept Drills: Applications of Integration

Test your conceptual understanding of the applications of integration.

## Conceptual Questions

1.  **True or False:** The area between $y = x^2$ and $y = -x^2$ from $x=-1$ to $x=1$ is zero because the integrals cancel out.
2.  If you revolve the region bounded by $y = x$ and $y = x^2$ around the x-axis, do you use the disk method or the washer method? Why?
3.  Why is the formula for arc length $L = \int \sqrt{1 + (f'(x))^2} dx$ derived using the Pythagorean theorem?
4.  In the formula for pumping water out of a tank, what does the term $(\rho g \cdot Area(y) \cdot dy)$ represent physically?
5.  Why is the area moment of inertia of a rectangular beam proportional to $h^3$ but only proportional to $b^1$?
6.  If the average value of a continuous function $f(x)$ on $[0, 10]$ is $5$, does $f(x)$ *have* to equal $5$ at some point between $0$ and $10$?

## Analytical Problems

1.  **Area:** Set up (do not evaluate) the integral to find the area of the region bounded by $y = \sin(x)$ and $y = \cos(x)$ between $x=0$ and $x=\pi/2$. (Hint: You must split the integral).
2.  **Volume (Washer):** Set up the integral for the volume generated by revolving the region bounded by $y = \sqrt{x}$ and $y = x^2$ around the x-axis.
3.  **Volume (Shells):** Set up the integral for the volume generated by revolving the region bounded by $y = \sqrt{x}$, $x=4$, and $y=0$ around the y-axis, using cylindrical shells.
4.  **Work:** A spring has a natural length of 20 cm. If a force of 40 N is required to keep it stretched to a length of 30 cm, how much work is done in stretching it from 20 cm to 40 cm? (Hint: find the spring constant $k$ first).
5.  **Average Value:** Find the average value of $f(x) = x^2$ on the interval $[0, 3]$.
6.  **Improper Integral:** Evaluate $\int_{1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^2} \, dx$. Does it converge or diverge?

---

## Solutions

### Conceptual Solutions

1.  **False.** Area is always positive. The formula is $\int (\text{top} - \text{bottom}) dx = \int [x^2 - (-x^2)] dx = \int 2x^2 dx$, which yields a positive area.
2.  **Washer method.** Because the region sits between $y=x$ and $y=x^2$, it does not sit flush against the x-axis. When revolved, there will be a hollow trumpet-shaped cavity in the center.
3.  Because the curve is approximated by tiny straight line segments $ds$. The hypotenuse of the triangle formed by $dx$ and $dy$ is $ds = \sqrt{dx^2 + dy^2}$, which factors into the arc length formula.
4.  It represents the **weight** of a very thin, horizontal slice of water at height $y$. ($\rho g$ is weight density, $Area(y) \cdot dy$ is the volume of the slice).
5.  Because the integral for $I_x$ is $\int y^2 dA$. The distance $y$ from the neutral axis is squared. Material placed further from the neutral axis (higher $h$) contributes quadratically to the stiffness, whereas making the beam wider just adds material linearly.
6.  **Yes.** This is guaranteed by the Mean Value Theorem for Integrals, provided the function is continuous.

### Analytical Solutions

1.  The curves intersect at $x = \pi/4$.
    $\int_{0}^{\pi/4} (\cos(x) - \sin(x)) \, dx + \int_{\pi/4}^{\pi/2} (\sin(x) - \cos(x)) \, dx$
2.  Top curve $R(x) = \sqrt{x}$, bottom curve $r(x) = x^2$. Intersect at $x=0, 1$.
    $V = \pi \int_{0}^{1} ((\sqrt{x})^2 - (x^2)^2) \, dx = \pi \int_{0}^{1} (x - x^4) \, dx$
3.  Radius $r=x$, Height $h=\sqrt{x}$.
    $V = \int_{0}^{4} 2\pi x (\sqrt{x}) \, dx = 2\pi \int_{0}^{4} x^{3/2} \, dx$
4.  First, find $k$. Displacement $x = 0.1$ m. $F = kx \implies 40 = k(0.1) \implies k = 400$ N/m.
    Work from $x=0$ to $x=0.2$ m:
    $W = \int_{0}^{0.2} 400x \, dx = [200x^2]_0^{0.2} = 200(0.04) = 8$ Joules.
5.  $f_{avg} = \frac{1}{3-0} \int_{0}^{3} x^2 \, dx = \frac{1}{3} \left[ \frac{x^3}{3} \right]_0^3 = \frac{1}{3} (9) = 3$.
6.  $\lim_{t \to \infty} \int_{1}^{t} x^{-2} \, dx = \lim_{t \to \infty} \left[ -\frac{1}{x} \right]_1^t = \lim_{t \to \infty} \left(-\frac{1}{t} - (-1)\right)$. As $t \to \infty$, $-1/t \to 0$. The limit is 1. The integral **converges** to 1.
