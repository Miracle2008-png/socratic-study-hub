# Enhanced Oil Recovery: Chemical Methods

Thermal EOR is incredible, but it only works on shallow, heavy oil fields. If your reservoir is 15,000 feet deep and the oil is light, pumping steam is useless (the steam will lose all its heat to the earth before it ever reaches the bottom of the well).

For deep, light-oil reservoirs suffering from poor waterflood sweep efficiency and trapped residual oil, engineers must alter the microscopic chemistry of the fluids themselves. 

This is **Chemical Enhanced Oil Recovery (CEOR)**. It involves pumping millions of gallons of exotic, expensive chemicals into the ground to fix the exact physical flaws of water.

## 1. Polymer Flooding: Fixing the Mobility Ratio

The greatest flaw of water is its incredibly low viscosity (1 cP). If you try to push a 50 cP oil with a 1 cP water, the water will instantly form "Viscous Fingers," stabbing through the oil and ruining the Areal Sweep Efficiency ($E_A$).

The solution is to simply make the water thicker.
*   Engineers dump massive quantities of high-tech, long-chain synthetic **Polymers** (like polyacrylamide) into the injection water on the surface.
*   These massive molecules act like microscopic nets, tangling together and radically increasing the viscosity of the water from 1 cP to 50 cP.
*   When this thick, syrupy "Polymer Water" is pumped into the ground, it matches the viscosity of the oil. 
*   Because the Mobility Ratio is now favorable ($M < 1$), the polymer water forms a solid, unstoppable wall, acting like a massive squeegee that pushes the oil uniformly across the entire reservoir without any fingering.

*The Catch:* Polymer molecules are massive and incredibly fragile. If they are pumped too violently through the surface pumps, or if they are forced through microscopic pore throats in tight rock, the long molecular chains will literally snap in half (shear degradation), instantly destroying the viscosity and ruining the \$10 million project.

## 2. Surfactant Flooding: Destroying Surface Tension

Polymer flooding improves sweep, but it cannot fix the Microscopic Displacement Efficiency ($E_D$). It cannot force the trapped, residual oil droplets ($S_{or}$) out of the tiny pore throats.

The oil droplets are trapped because of **Interfacial Tension (IFT)** and capillary forces. The water and oil repel each other so violently that the oil droplet acts like a rigid rubber ball that refuses to squeeze through the microscopic bottleneck.

The solution is industrial soap.
*   Engineers inject a massive slug of **Surfactants**. Surfactant molecules have a hydrophilic (water-loving) head and a lipophilic (oil-loving) tail.
*   These molecules aggressively embed themselves exactly on the boundary between the trapped oil and the water.
*   This chemically destroys the Interfacial Tension (reducing it by a factor of 10,000). 
*   The rigid oil droplet instantly becomes hyper-flexible and floppy. It easily slithers through the microscopic pore throat and joins the flowing stream.

## 3. Alkaline Flooding: Making Soap Underground

Surfactants are astronomically expensive. Pumping enough synthetic surfactant to flood a 100-square-mile oil field could bankrupt the project.

**Alkaline Flooding** is a brilliant chemical workaround.
Instead of buying expensive soap, the engineers simply pump incredibly cheap, highly caustic, high-pH chemicals (like Sodium Carbonate or Sodium Hydroxide) into the ground.

*   Many crude oils (especially heavy ones) naturally contain complex organic naphthenic acids. 
*   When the high-pH Alkaline water hits the crude oil deep underground, an intense chemical reaction occurs (Saponification).
*   The alkaline chemically reacts with the organic acids in the oil to literally **manufacture soap in-situ** (directly inside the rock).
*   This naturally generated surfactant then drops the interfacial tension and frees the trapped oil.

## 4. ASP Flooding: The Ultimate Chemical Weapon

Because each chemical fixes a different problem, modern CEOR utilizes all three simultaneously in an **Alkaline-Surfactant-Polymer (ASP) Flood**.

1.  **Alkaline** is pumped to generate natural soap and to neutralize the rock (preventing the rock from absorbing the expensive synthetic chemicals).
2.  A small dose of synthetic **Surfactant** is added to drastically lower the interfacial tension and release the trapped residual oil.
3.  **Polymer** is added to make the entire chemical slug thick and viscous, ensuring it sweeps evenly through the rock without fingering.

When executed perfectly, an ASP flood can sweep a reservoir so cleanly that the rock is left completely stripped of oil, achieving total recovery factors exceeding 70%.
